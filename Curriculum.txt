Grade 3
1. Operations & Algebraic Thinking
* Multiplication and division within 100
* Example problems: 5×7, 25÷5
* Properties of operations: commutative, associative, distributive
* Fluency: memorize single-digit products and related division facts
* Word problems: solve 2-step problems using +, −, ×, ÷

2. Number & Operations in Base 10
* Place value and rounding
* Round whole numbers to nearest 10 or 100
* Addition and subtraction within 1,000
* Multiplication by multiples of 10
* Understand digits in a four-digit number
* Thousands, hundreds, tens, ones; write in expanded form

3. Number & Operations — Fractions
* Understanding fractions as numbers
* Unit fractions (1/b) and a/b for denominators 2, 3, 4, 6, 8
* Fraction representation
* Show fractions on a number line
* Fraction equivalence and comparison
* Generate & recognize equivalent fractions (½ = 2/4); compare using >, =, <

4. Measurement & Data
* Time and elapsed time
* Tell/write time to the nearest minute; solve elapsed time word problems
* Volume and mass: measure in grams, kilograms, liters; solve one-step word problems
* Data displays: draw/interpret scaled pictographs & bar graphs; solve “how many more/less” problems
* Line plots: measure lengths to halves/quarters and plot on line plots
* Area and perimeter: recognize area as an attribute; measure by counting unit squares
* Relate area to multiplication; find areas of composite shapes
* Solve perimeter problems
* Explore same-area, different-perimeter rectangles

5. Geometry
* Shape attributes and classification
* Identify/classify shapes (triangles, quadrilaterals, pentagons, hexagons)
* Understand shared attributes (e.g., rhombus, rectangle, square are all quadrilaterals)

Grade 4
1. Operations & Algebraic Thinking
* Solve multi-step word problems with all four operations (including remainders)
* Interpret expressions flexibly (letters as unknowns, mental estimation)
* Study factors and multiples (1–100); categorize numbers as prime or composite
* Generate & analyze number/shape patterns; explain features like alternating odd/even

2. Number & Operations in Base Ten
* Understand place value up to 1,000,000
* Multiply & divide by powers of 10
* Read/write numbers in words, numerals, expanded form; compare using >, <, =
* Round multi-digit numbers to any place
* Perform multi-digit addition and subtraction using the standard algorithm
* Multiply four-digit numbers by one-digit
* Multiply two-digit by two-digit (place-value strategies & models)
* Divide four-digit dividends by one-digit divisors; interpret quotients & remainders

2. Number & Operations — Fractions
* Recognize equivalent fractions using visual models (denominators ≤100)
* Compare fractions with different numerators/denominators (benchmarks/common denominators)
* Build fractions via sums of unit fractions; add/subtract fractions referring to the same whole
* Convert fractions with denominator 10 to denominator 100 and add them
* Compare decimals to hundredths

3. Measurement & Data
* Convert within measurement systems: Metric (km–m–cm, kg–g, L–mL) and Customary (lb–oz, time units)
* Record in conversion tables
* Solve word problems using all operations on distances, time, volume, mass, and money
* Create/interpret line plots with fractional units (½, ¼, ⅛)
* Recognize and measure angles using a protractor (degrees); use additive angle properties

4. Geometry
* Draw & identify points, lines, line segments, rays, angles (right, acute, obtuse)
* Identify parallel/perpendicular lines in figures
* Classify shapes (triangles, quadrilaterals, etc.) based on side/angle properties

Grade 5
1. Operations & Algebraic Thinking
* Write and explain numerical expressions
* Analyze patterns & relationships

2. Number & Operations — Fractions
* Add/subtract fractions with unlike denominators using equivalent fractions
* Solve word problems involving addition/subtraction of fractions and mixed numbers
* Multiply fractions and whole numbers; multiply two fractions
* Divide unit fractions by whole numbers and vice versa

3. Number & Operations in Base Ten
* Understand decimals to thousandths (place value, operations)
* Compare, round, and perform operations with decimals

4. Measurement & Data
* Convert like measurement units (customary and metric)
* Represent and interpret data (line plots, volume models)

5. Geometry
* Graph points on the coordinate plane (Quadrant I)
* Classify two-dimensional figures by properties (parallel/perpendicular sides, angle measures)

Grade 6
1. Ratio & Proportional Reasoning
* Understand ratios, ratio language, and unit rates using whole numbers and simple fractions
* Solve problems via tables, tape diagrams, number lines, and coordinate graphs
* Compute percents as rates per 100; solve missing-value and unit price problems
* Convert measurement units using ratio reasoning (within metric or customary systems)

2. The Number System
* Divide fractions by fractions using models and equations
* Fluently divide multi-digit numbers and multi-digit decimals
* Add, subtract, multiply, and divide decimals to any place value
* Find GCF (≤100) and LCM (≤12); use distributive property
* Understand integers & rational numbers: opposites, ordering, absolute value
* Graph points in all four quadrants and find distances

3. Expressions & Equations
* Write and evaluate expressions with exponents, variables, and whole-number operations
* Identify parts of expressions (terms, factors, coefficients); use order of operations
* Apply properties (distributive, commutative, associative) to rewrite expressions
* Solve one-variable equations like x + p = q and interpret in context

4. Geometry
* Compute areas of triangles, quadrilaterals, and polygons using composition/decomposition
* Find volumes of right rectangular prisms with fractional edges using unit cubes and formulas V = l × w × h
* Draw polygons in coordinate plane; compute side lengths
* Use nets of prisms and pyramids to compute surface area
* Relate area and volume models to identify perfect squares and cubes

5. Statistics & Probability
* Recognize statistical questions and representative sampling
* Describe distributions via center, spread, and shape
* Represent data visually (dot plots, histograms) and summarize with mean, median, mode, range, IQR, and MAD
* Investigate simple probability models; compare predicted vs observed frequencies

Grade 7
1. Ratio & Proportional Reasoning
* Compute unit rates involving fractions (including different units, like speed)
* Recognize and represent proportional relationships via graphs, tables, and equations

2. The Number System
* Add, subtract, multiply, and divide integers and rational numbers
* Use integer arithmetic in context; extend operations to rational numbers

3. Expressions & Equations
* Solve word problems leading to linear equations of form p x + q = r and inequalities p x + q ≥ r
* Graph inequality solutions on number lines; express variable ranges

4. Geometry
* Construct and describe geometric figures and measure angles (right, acute, obtuse)
* Solve volume problems involving right triangular prisms and right rectangular prisms

5. Statistics & Probability
* Construct and interpret box plots; compute interquartile range; identify outliers
* Design and evaluate probability models

Grade 8
1. Expressions & Equations
* Solve linear equations, including formulas like perimeters and areas
* Solve pairs of linear equations (systems) via inspection, tables, or graphs
* Use variables in functions: understand y = m x + b; compare functions in multiple representations

2. Functions
* Define functions as rules mapping inputs to outputs; understand their graphs as ordered pairs
* Compare function properties across tables, graphs, or verbal descriptions

3. Geometry
* Define angles, circles, parallel & perpendicular lines, line segments
* Represent transformations: translations, rotations, reflections; analyze symmetry
* Explore slopes of parallel/perpendicular lines; apply in coordinate solutions
* Use coordinates to compute perimeters and areas; find segment points partitioned by ratio
