Grade 3
1. Multiplication & Division
* Practice multiplying and dividing numbers up to 100
* Learn tricks to help (like flip-order or groups)
* Learn facts like 5×7 or 25÷5
* Memorize simple facts like 6×8 = 48
* Solve word problems using +, −, ×, ÷
2. Place Value & Number Sense
* Understand place value (like 3,482 = 3,000 + 400 + 80 + 2)
* Round numbers to the nearest 10 or 100
* Add and subtract up to 1,000
* Multiply by 10, 20, etc.
* Learn about thousands, hundreds, tens, and ones
3. Fractions
* Understand fractions like ½ or ¾
* Show fractions on number lines
* See which fractions are the same (½ = 2/4)
* Compare fractions (is ¾ bigger than ⅔?)
4. Measurement & Data
* Tell time to the minute; figure out how much time passed
* Measure weight and liquid (grams, liters)
* Make bar graphs and pictographs
* Use line plots with fractions
* Learn about area and perimeter
* Find area using squares or multiplication
* Solve real-world problems with area and perimeter
5. Shapes & Geometry
* Name and describe shapes like squares and triangles
* Sort shapes by their sides and corners

Grade 4
1. Operations & Patterns
* Solve tricky word problems using all 4 operations
* Work with unknown numbers (like 3 × __ = 12)
* Understand factors and multiples (like 5 is a factor of 20)
* Find patterns in numbers and shapes
2. Big Numbers & Place Value
* Understand numbers up to 1 million
* Multiply and divide by 10, 100, etc.
* Write numbers in different ways
* Round any number
* Do big addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division problems
3. Fractions & Decimals
* Use pictures to understand fractions
* Compare and add fractions (even with different bottoms)
* Add decimals like 0.4 + 0.6 = 1.0
4. Measurement & Data
* Change between units (like cm to m or minutes to hours)
* Solve real-world problems with time, weight, and money
* Use line plots with fractions
* Measure angles with a protractor
5. Geometry
* Know parts of shapes: points, lines, angles
* Identify right, acute, and obtuse angles
* Sort shapes based on sides and angles

Grade 5
1. Expressions & Patterns
* Write math expressions and explain them
* Find and describe number patterns
2. Fractions
* Add and subtract fractions with different bottoms
* Multiply fractions
* Divide simple fractions (like ½ ÷ 3 or 4 ÷ ½)
3. Decimals
* Work with decimals to the thousandths
* Compare, round, and calculate with decimals
4. Measurement & Data
* Convert between units (cm, m, g, kg)
* Use line plots and find volume of boxes
5. Geometry
* Graph points on a grid
* Sort shapes by sides and angles

Grade 6
1. Ratios & Percents
* Understand and use ratios (like 3:1)
* Solve problems with rates and unit price
* Use percent (like 20% of 50)
* Change units using ratios
2. Numbers
* Divide fractions by fractions
* Do all operations with big numbers and decimals
* Understand positive and negative numbers
* Graph numbers on a number line
3. Expressions & Equations
* Use variables (like x)
* Know math rules like order of operations
* Solve equations like x + 5 = 12
4. Geometry
* Find area and volume of shapes
* Use grids to draw shapes and find distances
* Unfold 3D shapes into nets
5. Statistics
* Ask good data questions
* Make and read dot plots and histograms
* Understand averages and spreads
* Learn simple probability

Grade 7
1. Proportional Relationships
* Solve problems with unit rates (like 3 miles in ½ hour)
* Use tables and graphs to show relationships
2. Numbers
* Work with all rational numbers (positive and negative)
* Use math with real-world contexts
3. Equations & Inequalities
* Solve word problems that lead to equations
* Graph inequalities on number lines
4. Geometry
* Build and measure shapes
* Solve volume problems
5. Data & Probability
* Use box plots and find outliers
* Build simple models for probability

Grade 8
1. Equations & Systems
* Solve all kinds of linear equations
* Solve systems of two equations
* Understand functions like y = mx + b
2. Functions
* Know how inputs go to outputs
* Use tables, graphs, and rules to describe functions
3. Geometry
* Learn about angles, lines, circles
* Transform shapes (move, flip, turn)
* Use coordinates to find perimeter and area

SHSAT
1. Basic Math
* Do all operations with whole numbers, fractions, and decimals
* Use GCF and LCM
* Solve problems with ratios and percentages
2. Algebra
* Solve equations and simplify expressions
* Translate word problems to equations
3. Geometry
* Find area, perimeter, and volume
* Work with angles and graphs
4. Word Problems
* Solve tricky real-world problems (speed, time, logic)
5. Data
* Read charts and graphs
* Understand averages and basic probability

SAT
1. Algebra
* Solve and graph linear equations
* Work with systems and word problems
2. Data Analysis
* Use ratios, percentages, and units
* Read data from charts and graphs
3. Advanced Math
* Solve quadratic and exponential equations
* Understand functions and graphs
4. Geometry & Trig
* Use formulas for area, volume, and angles
* Basic trigonometry with right triangles
5. Calculator Skills
* Know when to use a calculator or mental math
